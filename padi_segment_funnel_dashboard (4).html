How this data was collected — Referrer-based segment methodology
Each region is a GA4 segment defined as: page referrer contains padi.com/[locale] (e.g. padi.com/de for German). This captures users whose session on any PADI subdomain was referred from a regional locale page — meaning they actively clicked through. This is a self-selected, higher-intent audience than all site visitors.
padi.com/de → click → any PADI subdomain → lands on → specific page → purchase or drop-off
⚠️ "(not set)" pages indicate sessions where the referrer chain broke (direct/app traffic) — these inflate bounce rates and are flagged per region.

Context

All 11 regional segments combined = 14.0% of global www.padi.com traffic. The other 86% never cross into a subdomain via a regional locale referrer.

Key insight
DE alone is 3.41% of all global PADI traffic. Top 3 (DE, ES, FR) = 7.8% of global but drive the majority of regional purchases.
🏆 DE dominates volume
DE generates 1,826 purchases — more than ES + FR combined. With 87K entry users it's the largest regional segment and drives the most downstream traffic to store and travel.
💡 AR & TH — hidden quality
AR users average 741s per session — nearly 10 sessions per user. TH and KO also show 78–80% engagement. These are small but highly committed audiences systematically undervalued.
⚠️ Dive Shops — #1 destination
The top landing page after click-through is /[locale]/dive-shops/nearby/ in nearly every region. Users are looking for dive shops, not buying courses. A discovery gap exists between intent and conversion.
📈 ES leads travel revenue
ES generates 561 travel purchases at 3.87% conversion — the highest travel conv rate of all regions and more travel purchases than DE (372). ES users are disproportionately travel-intent.
🚨 (not set) tracking gap
56,796 users across 11 regions have unresolved "(not set)" landing pages. ZH-HANT (20.9%), AR (20%), ZH-HANS (19.2%) are worst affected. These sessions show artificially high bounce rates.
🔄 Multi-session behaviour
Sessions far exceed users in every region. AR: 9.3 sessions/user, TH: 8.4, ZH-HANS: 3.6. These aren't one-time visitors — they are returning audiences that the old hostname data couldn't see.

What this view shows
Each region's landing page engagement quality (avg engagement rate across top pages) is plotted against its store conversion rate (purchases ÷ users who reached store). Regions in the top-right quadrant have both high-quality post-click pages AND strong conversion — they are the benchmark. Regions bottom-right have good conversion despite lower LP quality — opportunity to improve further. Top-left have engaged pages but conversion leakage elsewhere in the funnel.
