All Funnels — Comparative View

Closed funnel approach — users must complete steps in sequence from Step 1. Comparing purchase volume, drop-off patterns and conversion efficiency across all 6 funnels.

Methodology — Closed Funnel

All 6 funnels use the closed funnel approach in GA4. This means users are only counted if they enter at Step 1 and progress through steps in sequence — mid-funnel entries are excluded. This gives a stricter, more conservative view of the journey: the numbers represent users who followed the exact intended path. Drop-off rates are therefore higher than an open funnel would show, but they more accurately reflect the true sequential conversion performance of each path.

Biggest leak point

Travel ATC→Purchase in discovery traffic converts at just 7.22% — 92.78% of users who add to cart on travel.padi.com do not complete. Investigate checkout friction and off-domain payment drop-off.

Strongest performer

Store direct funnel (Store → View → ATC → Purchase) converts 54.4% of store visitors to product views and achieves the highest purchase volume at 88,982 — by far the most efficient path.

Discovery traffic gap

Discovery engine sends 85,647 users to padi.com but only 1,541 purchase (1,367 store + 174 travel). That's a 1.8% end-to-end rate. The padi.com → intent domain step loses 55–68K users.

Store vs Travel intent

In all-traffic funnels, Store delivers 57,010 purchases vs 8,048 for Travel — 7× more. But Travel direct funnel shows 19.98% view-to-ATC rate vs 15.7% for Store, suggesting Travel browsers have higher intent once on-domain.
